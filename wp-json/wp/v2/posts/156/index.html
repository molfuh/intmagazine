Videos from Audiences Across the World<\/p>\n\n\n\n

From Spain to Ireland, the Philippines to Canada, The Lady of Heaven movie trailer has picked
up exponentially. The Enlightened Kingdom film production company released the first trailer for
their historical drama, ‘The Lady of Heaven’ on Film Selects the YouTube Trailer platform.
The trailer has gained reaction videos from countries everywhere, including from the UK,
Thailand, India, Poland, Netherlands, Romania, Brazil, Kenya, Jamaica, Iceland, Russia,
Denmark, New Zealand, Algeria, Italy, Georgia, Morocco, Malaysia, and Cyprus.
Since then, The Lady of Heaven trailer has caught the viewer’s attention, with views reaching
nearly two million in under a month. With thousands of reposts already, The Lady of Heaven
trailer’s comments section was garnering close to one hundred thousand before being turned
off.<\/p>\n\n\n\n


There have been over 1,000 reaction videos to The Lady of Heaven trailer, and the number of
videos popping up is growing every single day. Naturally, the trailer release has generated a
significant amount of excitement online, generating reposts worldwide, Instagram accounts
based on the film, and buildup on social media rarely seen for a non-studio film.
The phenomenal reaction from The Lady of Heaven\u2019s fan base could be in part due to the
quality of the filmmaking and the story of the film itself. The film covers a story and a multitude
of themes that have never been discussed previously on the big screens. The nature of the film
itself is causing audiences to share what they have seen and to talk about their experiences
watching the trailer.
The Lady of Heaven covers two storylines simultaneously throughout the duration of the film.
On one end, the plight of an Iraqi child who tragically loses his mother during the war, and on
the other end, the agonizing journey of the Lady Fatima, daughter of the Prophet Muhammad.
The Lady of Heaven depicts two plots separated by 1400 years. First, Laith’s story as an orphan
growing up amidst a war-torn country, who learns the lesson of power and patience. Second,
Lady Fatima’s story as the first victim of terrorism and the terrors she had to endure.
“The Lady is held dear in the hearts of billions worldwide, but her story has never been shared
through film. Her story is a universal one that we can all learn from, teaching us the best
response to corruption and to power hungry politicians,” the filmmakers say.
This message has become highly relevant, especially today, where the media is causing people
to look at what politicians are doing daily. It is not a coincidence that audiences feel compelled
to share their reactions to the film’s trailer or that there have been over 1,000 fan reaction
videos made.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
