Three years since its debut, \u201cThe Divide\u201d continues to captivate audiences, film critics, and
many more. From winning 26 out of 38 award nominations to securing invitations to over 15 film
festivals, the film has accumulated quite a collection of accolades and has made quite the name
for itself all across the world.<\/p>\n\n\n\n


\u201cThe Divide\u201d stars some of the most recognizable names in Hollywood \u2013 to call it star-studded
would truly be an understatement. The film stars Bryan Kaplan (Chronicles of Jessica Wu, Cult
of Eden), Sara Arrington (Stargirl, The Jungle Book), Levi Kreis (A Very Sordid Wedding, Don’t
Let Go), Luke Colombero (Operation: Neighborhood Watch!, The Pretty One) and Perry King
(The Day After Tomorrow, The Hasty Heart) as well. If this is not enough to impress viewers,
then Jana Brown\u2019s writing will be.<\/p>\n\n\n\n


Brown, a graduate of Dartmouth College, has amassed her own collection of awards and
honors for her enthralling storytelling. She has won \u201cBest Foreign Feature,\u201d \u201cBest First Time
Screenwriter,\u201d \u201cBest Modern Western Screenplay,\u201d \u201cBest First Time Feature Filmmakers,\u201d and
has even won the International Independent Film Awards’ coveted \u201cSilver Award – Screenplay.\u201d
However, while she has clearly found great success in screenplay writing, her first breakout
piece was actually \u201cThe Divide.\u201d Before getting into screenplay writing, Brown had spent much
of her career in the publishing and print industry. Later on, she worked as a freelance content
creator until she found her calling and took on \u201cThe Divide.\u201d Since the film\u2019s incredible debut,
Brown has also written two books and begun work on a number of new screenplays.<\/p>\n\n\n\n


Perry King makes up the other half of this dynamic duo that brought \u201cThe Divide\u201d to incredible
heights. King portrayed Sam Kincaide in the film and also worked behind the scenes as the
director of \u201cThe Divide.\u201d Though King is a veteran of the entertainment industry, having acted in
a number of films, this film marked the first time he was ever behind the camera. While he
lacked director experience, the accolades he received for his work would say otherwise \u2013 King
received \u201cBest First Time Director,\u201d \u201cPlatinum Remi Award for Best Western,\u201d \u201cSilver Award,
Narrative Feature,\u201d \u201cBest Dramatic Feature,\u201d and \u201cDebut Filmmaker Award.\u201d<\/p>\n\n\n\n


\u201cThe Divide\u201d is filmed entirely in black and white, mixing modernity and class to portray the story
of one man\u2019s struggles with various droughts in his life. “I can’t imagine [The Divide] being
anything but black and white. The old black and white films, those are what I love the most,”
said King in a recent interview. King finds a way to beautifully encapsulate the themes of classic
American westerns, modern cinematography, and black and white filmmaking in a single film.
The storyline is set in 1976 and follows a drought-plagued ranch in Northern California in an
area only known as \u201cthe divide.\u201d Sam Kincaid (King) is waging a losing battle against
Alzheimer\u2019s disease, a barren landscape, and a tragic past. After realizing that this battle will
only end one way, a migrant ranch hand (Kaplan) enlists the help of Sam\u2019s estranged daughter<\/p>\n\n\n\n

(Sara Arrington) to care for him at the ranch. Her arrival uncovers a long and dark family history,
highlighting the consequences of absence.<\/p>\n\n\n\n


Over the course of the film, Kincaid attempts to reconcile with his daughter, his grandson
(Colembero) seeks a father figure, and Tom Cutler (Kreis) embarks on a mission to settle the
score with Sam. The many droughts in the divide are unrelenting, and Kincaid\u2019s daughter Sarah
maintains a similar attitude as she seeks out peace of mind in the wake of her father\u2019s past
failures. “Drought of the land, drought of your family and your personal life. And for this old man
I play, drought of the mind,” commented King.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
