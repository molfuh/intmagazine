    The Lady of Heaven Movie Trailer Generates Record-Breaking 1,000 YouTube Reaction

    By molfuh

    Videos from Audiences Across the World

    From Spain to Ireland, the Philippines to Canada, The Lady of Heaven movie trailer has picked
    up exponentially. The Enlightened Kingdom film production company released the first trailer for
    their historical drama, ‘The Lady of Heaven’ on Film Selects the YouTube Trailer platform.
    The trailer has gained reaction videos from countries everywhere, including from the UK,
    Thailand, India, Poland, Netherlands, Romania, Brazil, Kenya, Jamaica, Iceland, Russia,
    Denmark, New Zealand, Algeria, Italy, Georgia, Morocco, Malaysia, and Cyprus.
    Since then, The Lady of Heaven trailer has caught the viewer’s attention, with views reaching
    nearly two million in under a month. With thousands of reposts already, The Lady of Heaven
    trailer’s comments section was garnering close to one hundred thousand before being turned
    off.


    There have been over 1,000 reaction videos to The Lady of Heaven trailer, and the number of
    videos popping up is growing every single day. Naturally, the trailer release has generated a
    significant amount of excitement online, generating reposts worldwide, Instagram accounts
    based on the film, and buildup on social media rarely seen for a non-studio film.
    The phenomenal reaction from The Lady of Heavenâ€™s fan base could be in part due to the
    quality of the filmmaking and the story of the film itself. The film covers a story and a multitude
    of themes that have never been discussed previously on the big screens. The nature of the film
    itself is causing audiences to share what they have seen and to talk about their experiences
    watching the trailer.
    The Lady of Heaven covers two storylines simultaneously throughout the duration of the film.
    On one end, the plight of an Iraqi child who tragically loses his mother during the war, and on
    the other end, the agonizing journey of the Lady Fatima, daughter of the Prophet Muhammad.
    The Lady of Heaven depicts two plots separated by 1400 years. First, Laith’s story as an orphan
    growing up amidst a war-torn country, who learns the lesson of power and patience. Second,
    Lady Fatima’s story as the first victim of terrorism and the terrors she had to endure.
    “The Lady is held dear in the hearts of billions worldwide, but her story has never been shared
    through film. Her story is a universal one that we can all learn from, teaching us the best
    response to corruption and to power hungry politicians,” the filmmakers say.
    This message has become highly relevant, especially today, where the media is causing people
    to look at what politicians are doing daily. It is not a coincidence that audiences feel compelled
    to share their reactions to the film’s trailer or that there have been over 1,000 fan reaction
    videos made.

    “The Lady is a powerful and important historical character that many in the world have never
    been introduced to. The film is also exceptionally historically accurate. We worked tirelessly to
    ensure that all aspects of the film are linked to historical records, from the characters, to the
    scenes, to clothing, armoury, pottery, houses, etc. Everything was painstakingly researched,”
    the filmmakers say.
    In Iraq, the film’s trailer has gained massive support and has been overwhelmingly positive, due
    in part to the Iraqi heritage represented in its storyline. Across the country, the people have
    taken it upon themselves to print out movie posters for the film and plaster them across the
    streets to support the film.
    Fans of The Lady of Heaven have downloaded the trailer from its YouTube platform and
    projected it on big screens at intersections across all the main cities in Iraq.
    “There are 250 films on Jesus Christ, 120 films about Moses, 80 about the other Prophets, and
    40 films about Buddha, but there are none about the life of Lady Fatima the Daughter of the
    Prophet Muhammed, until now,” Hussain Ashmere, Executive Producer says.
    We asked, why now? Why did you choose to create this story and release it as a feature film
    during these times?


    “In a time of global uncertainty and hardship, the Lady’s story is a much-needed message of
    patience, hope, and strength,” Abdul-Malik Shlibak, Executive Producer, said.
    “This film conveys a message of love and peace. It is a call to a better mindset when dealing
    with challenges. I am certain that should humanity follow in the steps of The Lady, peace,
    justice, and equality will prevail and triumph. I pray for this to happen,” the film’s writer Sheikh
    Al-Habib adds.
    One of the main challenges that the filmmakers had to overcome was accurately depicting holy
    figures without disrespecting religious codes. Thankfully, with newly developed technology,
    audiences will be able to see that these elements have been executed thoughtfully in respect of
    the religious topics in the film.
    Producers of the film state, “Following Islamic tradition, no individual represented a Holy
    Personality during the making of this film. The performances of the Holy Personalities were
    achieved through a unique synthesis of actors, in-camera effects, lighting, and visual effects.”
    With a budget of $15 Million, filmed in Georgia and London, England, the movie is described by
    John Stephenson OBE, Creative Consultant for the film, as “Lawrence of Arabia meets Game of
    Thrones.”

    Filmmakers have been ecstatic with the responses towards the film since its release last
    December, despite the restrictions that the pandemic has previously placed on them. We can
    expect to see more of this groundbreaking film in the future.
